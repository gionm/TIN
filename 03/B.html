HTTPS

In its popular operation on the Internet, HTTPS provides authentication ofthe website and associated web server with which one of the parties is communicating, protecting the communication from known attacks through the man in the middle technique.

Furthermore, HTTPS provides a two-way encryption of communications between a client and a server , which protects the same against possible eavesdropping operations , (an action by which the private conversation between the parties is secretly listened without their consent ) and tampering ( literally tampering with or alteration of the communication ) falsifying its contents.

In practice, this mechanism provides a satisfactory guarantee that you are communicating exactly with the desired website (as opposed to a fake website), as well as ensuring that the contents of the communications between the user and the website are not can be intercepted or altered by third parties.

Historically, HTTPS connections were primarily used for payments in World Wide Web transactions , e-mails, and for sensitive transactions within corporate information systems . In the late 2000s and early 2010s, HTTPS began to have widespread and widespread use to protect the authenticity of web pages , the security of user accounts, and to keep the communications, identity and web browsing of the web private.
